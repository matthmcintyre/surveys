Consent Form

What is the study about?

The goal of the AATD Case Series Study is to understand the clinical benefit of the 23andMe Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD) Genetic Health Risk report for customers. This consent document serves as a supplement to our full research consent document here

Who can participate?

You can participate in this Study if you:

    • Have previously taken the 23andMe AATD Research Survey;
    • Have viewed your AATD Genetic Health Risk report;
    • Have provided consent to participate in 23andMe Research;
    • Are not participating in 23andMe Research via a legally authorized representative (LAR);
    • Are 18 years old or older; and,
    • Live in the United States.

What am I agreeing to if I consent?

    • You have already agreed to let 23andMe researchers use your Genetic & Self-Reported Information for Research, as described in the main 23andMe Research consent here.

Additionally, if you agree to this consent document, you agree to:

    • Provide us with your email and availability so we can schedule an interview with you.
    • Participate in an interview about your experience related to the 23andMe AATD Genetic Health Risk report; this interview should take approximately 60 minutes to complete. We may ask you for your permission to record the interview, but it is your choice whether or not the interview is recorded. We may also use third-party platforms, such as Zoom and Google Form, to determine your eligibility, and schedule and conduct your interviews. You are allowed to skip questions you do not want to answer and you may end the interview at any time.

How will my data be used?

Information you share will be used to understand how people use and benefit from the 23andMe AATD Genetic Health Risk report. We may share our findings from this study with the public, including anonymous quotes from the interviews we conduct. Any findings or quotes we publish will not include your name or any other personally identifying information.

How do you keep my data protected and confidential for this study?

23andMe uses a range of physical, technical, and administrative procedures to protect the privacy of your Personal Information, including your Genetic Information and Self-Reported Information (e.g. survey or interview responses). 23andMe study investigators aim to minimize the chances of a breach by following various policies and procedures, including the ones listed below:

    • Your name and contact information may be used to communicate with you but are not analyzed in combination with your interview data or your Genetic Information.
    • Interview data will be temporarily stored on password-protected servers. Once interview data collection is complete, the data will be transferred and permanently stored in a secure research computing environment.
    • If 23andMe, 23andMe research collaborators, or future researchers publish data (for example in a peer-reviewed journal, blog post or other medium), only data summaries, de-identified interview responses, or combined data from many individuals, will be published.
    • All 23andMe employees are trained on how to work with human research participants. In addition, all 23andMe study investigators are trained on how to conduct research responsibly.

What are the benefits and risks?

The benefits and risks associated with this Study are the same as those described in our Main Research Consent document here. The procedures for this study do not involve more than the minimal risks. There may be additional risks to participation that are currently unforeseeable. There may be an indirect benefit to you as knowledge about the impact of 23andMe reports on customers increases.

Do I have any alternatives? Can I withdraw from this study?

    • Your participation in this research is completely voluntary. Your alternative is not to participate in the Study. If you choose not to participate, your status as a 23andMe customer will not be impacted and access to your account will not be changed.
    • If you change your mind, you can withdraw from this study at any time by emailing the study team at aatd-study@23andme.com. You can also return to this consent page and change your consent. The link to this study consent page is available in the study email that you received. If you withdraw, 23andMe will prevent your information from being used in new 23andMe Research for the AATD Case Series Study occurring after 30 days from receipt of withdrawal (it may take up to 30 days to withdraw your information after you withdraw your consent). Any research on your data that has been performed or published prior to this date will not be reversed, undone, or withdrawn.

Is there a cost to participate? Will I be compensated?

    • There is no cost to participate.
    • After completion of the study activity, study participants will receive a gift certificate for Amazon with a cash value of US $75.

Who do I contact if I have questions?

If you have any questions or concerns about the Study, or if you suffer a study-related injury, please contact aatd-study@23andme.com .
