SURVEY TITLE: You could make a difference in sleep research
SURVEY DESCRIPTION: Join a first-of-its kind study that aims to understand how to improve sleep.

Do you not sleep as well as you would like? Are you unsatisfied with the quality of your sleep? We're interested in finding out whether genetics has something to do with that.

And we need your help.

For this study, we are looking to recruit committed 23andMe participants who are willing to change aspects of their lifestyle.


[1] PAGE: Study Information Page

1.

Here's how it works:


  • Enroll today: We will ask you to consent to participate in the study and take a set of surveys about your current sleep experience.

  • First 2 weeks starting the last week of September:

    • We will ask you to fill out sleep diary surveys for two weeks.

  • Next 4 weeks ending early November:

    • Commit to a change: We will randomly assign you to a study group and ask you to change your behavior for 4 weeks.

      • One study group focuses on daytime changes, like drinking less caffeine, exercising more or taking less naps.

      • The other study group focuses on nighttime changes, like having a consistent bedtime, reducing screen time before bed and practicing mindfulness before bed.

    • Answer surveys: We will ask you to answer surveys to see how your sleep quality changes over these 4 weeks.

Why your help is so important
This is the first study in which 23andMe is asking research participants to change their behavior for a period of time and log the outcomes. This can unlock a whole new range of possible discoveries. We hypothesize that different strategies for improving sleep may work better for certain individuals depending on genetics and other lifestyle factors. Understanding these connections could lead to new insights that could help people improve their sleep quality.

Who can enroll:

Individuals who meet all of the following criteria:

    • Are experiencing some insomnia, expressed as trouble falling asleep or staying asleep, resulting in broad dissatisfaction with sleep quality or quantity (but not experiencing other sleeping disorder(s), such as jet lag, parasomnia, narcolepsy, hypersomnia, etc.)
    • Live in the US and are between 18 and 65 years
    • Not under a doctor's care for any sleep disorder or other condition requiring bed rest
    • Have quiet, comfortable home environment without sleeping disruptions (e.g. young children at home, unusual noises, illness, pregnancy or other temporary interruptions)
    • Are unlikely to travel to different time zones during the study
    • Have a normal sleep time that is in the evening (8pm to 8am)
    • Do not use sleeping pills, melatonin or other sleep inducing substances or medications

FAQ

Can you tell me more about the Sleep Study survey questions?
Surveys will mostly include questions about your sleep in the past 24 hours or in the past week. We will also ask about your sleep quality in the past month and lifestyle habits.

Our goal is to enroll 10,000 participants.

We would like to know about that too! Sometimes change is hard and by continuing to complete surveys, we may be able to understand why certain changes are especially difficult for certain people.

Consent Document for Sleep Study

This consent document provides information relevant to the Sleep Study.

Why is this research study being done?

This research study aims to learn more about the relationship between genetics, behavior, and sleep quality. Previous research has shown that specific behaviors may improve sleep quality for those that have trouble sleeping. However, scientists need to know more about how genetics and environment affect the way those with trouble sleeping respond to a given behavioral change.

Your data may assist 23andMe researchers in activities including, but not limited to: developing and validating new strategies to predict and prevent sleep trouble.

We are recruiting current 23andMe research participants to take part in this research, including those who have reported that they have trouble sleeping as well as those who have not. Taking part in this study is completely voluntary. If you chose not to participate, there will be no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are entitled.

What am I agreeing to if I consent to this part of the research?

This form describes the research activities that are part of the 23andMe Sleep Study.

If you agree to participate in this study, you agree:

  • To complete surveys on the 23andMe website that will include questions about your sleep, lifestyle, and other habits. The surveys should take about 10 minutes on average.

  • Commit to changing at least one thing in your lifestyle for four weeks to attempt to improve your sleep. You will be randomly assigned to one of two study arms, daytime or nighttime. The daytime arm includes changes like drinking less caffeine, exercising more, or taking fewer naps. The nighttime arm includes changes like having a consistent bedtime, reducing screen time before bed, and engaging in meditation or mindfulness. We will ask you to make a change corresponding to the study arm you are assigned that makes sense for your lifestyle. We will ask you to tell us beforehand what you plan to change and then keep us informed of how well you are able to stick to that plan.

  • We may also invite you to contribute additional data through our mobile app. This activity is voluntary and if invited, we will provide additional information and consent.

The 23andMe Sleep Study is open-ended: we expect initial study involvement to last approximately six weeks, but data analysis will be ongoing and we may add new surveys or features in the future. If new surveys or features are added, you may receive an email update or see an announcement when you sign in to your account.

How do you keep my data protected and confidential?

23andMe uses a range of physical, technical, and administrative procedures to protect the privacy of your personal information, including your genetic data and your survey responses. The 23andMe researchers who have access to your survey responses and genetic data do not have access to Registration Information (name, address, email address, user ID, password, or credit card) of customers. Although 23andMe cannot provide a 100% guarantee that your data will be safe, 23andMe policies and procedures minimize the chance that a data breach could take place.

Certain regulatory agencies may also need access to study records, such as the Institutional Review Board (IRB) in case of an audit or for monitoring.

What are the potential benefits and risk?

Benefits: There may be no direct benefit of participating. There is a chance that this research will contribute to an increase in knowledge about sleep and/or methods for improving sleep quality.

Risks: The lifestyle changes that you make as part of this study may cause you discomfort. For example, withdrawing caffeine, increased exercise, or reduced naps may cause headaches or drowsiness. Having a consistent bedtime and reducing screen time may cause scheduling changes that affect your ability to participate in social situations and other enjoyable activities. In addition, it may be difficult to follow through with the lifestyle change that you committed to, which may cause stress. You can mitigate these risks by withdrawing from this study at any time.

In the event of an emergency, dial 911 immediately. If you require emergency care, be sure to tell the emergency care provider about your participation in this study.

Is there a cost to participate?

There is no cost for participating in this study.

You and your family will not receive any financial benefits or compensation from or any rights in any developments, inventions, or other discoveries that might come out of this research.

Do I have any alternatives? Can I withdraw from this study?

Your alternative is to not participate in this study. This will have no effect on your access to your genetic information or to the 23andMe Personal Genome Service.

Your participation is voluntary, and you may choose to withdraw from the study at any time and there will be no penalty or loss of benefits to which you are entitled. You can withdraw your self-reported information from this 23andMe Sleep Study by changing your consent status within the 23andMe "Settings" page.

If you withdraw from this study or are withdrawn by the Investigator, 23andMe will prevent the requested information from being used in new 23andMe research occurring after 30 days from receipt of withdrawal (it may take up to 30 days to withdraw your information after you withdraw your consent). Any research on your data that has been performed or published prior to this date will not be reversed, undone, or withdrawn.
