SURVEY TITLE: Color Vision
SURVEY DESCRIPTION: The Ishihara is a color-perception test designed to detect red-green colorblindness. It consists of a series of circular plates made up of smaller circles of varying sizes, which depict a unique pattern on each plate.

This survey should take 3-4 minutes to complete.

Images derived from Eight Ishihara charts for testing colour blindness, Europe, 1917-1959' by Science Museum, London. CC BY/ Cropped from original
This activity is not a diagnostic test. If you have concerns about your vision as a result of this activity, you should seek assistance from a professional eye care provider. Additionally, 23andMe does not cover health care costs, thus the cost of any vision-related care is the individual's responsibility.

Before you start:

You will be shown a total of 8 images. You will be asked to identify the number you see within each individual image.

This is a timed activity:

    • You will have a total of 3 seconds to view each image before you are asked to give your answer.
    • If you finish viewing the image before 3 seconds are up, you may hit 'Continue' to proceed and give your answer.

What to do if you are unable to see a number within an image:

    • Please enter the number '0' as your answer if you do not see a number within a particular image shown to you on your screen.
