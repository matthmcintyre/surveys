SURVEY TITLE: Bone Break Risk Assessment
SURVEY DESCRIPTION: According to the National Osteoporosis Foundation, approximately one in two women over the age of 50 in the US will have an osteoporosis-related bone break in their lifetime. This risk assessment survey, created by American Bone Health, is a collaboration with Amgen to help you understand the risk of breaking a bone in the next 10 years for a group of women like you and will be used to drive 23andMe's research. Note that some questions in this survey are required to calculate a bone break risk score and cannot be skipped. If you want to exit this survey, you may do so by closing your browser or by navigating elsewhere in the 23andMe website.

Most people finish this survey in about 3-5 minutes.
*More about the measure: The American Bone Health 10-Year Fracture Risk Calculator™ (ABH FRC) Version 2.1 estimates the 10-year risk for breaking a bone in any of four places (hip, wrist, arm, spine) for postmenopausal women and men age 45 and older who are not receiving treatment for osteoporosis.1

Disclaimer: Your participation is for research purposes only. The ABH FRC does not diagnose osteoporosis and has not been clinically validated. The results should not be used for diagnostic or medical purposes but can be used to start a conversation with your doctor. Consult with your doctor if you have questions or concerns about your risk for breaking a bone or osteoporosis.

1American Bone Health, About the ABH Fracture Risk Calculator Version 2.1 https://americanbonehealth.org/about-the-abh-fracture-risk-calculator-version-2-1/, Accessed 1/16/19.
