Why am I seeing this document?

The survey you are about to view contains topics that some people may see as sensitive. The survey you are about to view is part of a research project to look for genetic correlates of response to common antidepressant medications. The survey contains questions concerning sexual desire and sexual function, which some of these medications may affect. These topics may be highly personal and difficult to discuss. Answering some of the survey questions may cause emotional discomfort. In addition, scientific publication of genetic associations with specific behaviors or personal characteristics may change the way those behaviors or characteristics are perceived by the public. In some cases, such publication may affect the way you or groups to which you belong are perceived.

Participation in this research is completely voluntary, and you may stop answering this survey at any point, for any reason. Please note that due to the sensitive nature of this topic, persons under the age of 18 are not eligible for this study.

This survey contains some highly personal questions. You may wish to ensure that you are alone in a private place before continuing on to this survey. We also encourage you to keep your sign in password private to prevent others from accessing your responses to this and other surveys that you may not wish to share.

Please note that you can decline to answer any questions you do not wish to answer.

This document serves as an addition to our general research consent form, and provides additional information specific to this particular survey. The general research consent form, which can be viewed here, includes a more complete summary of the risks and benefits of participating in our research.

Whom do I contact if I have questions?

If you have any questions or concerns about this study, if you suffer a research-related injury, or if you have a question about subjects' rights, please call the Human Protections Administrator at 650.963.8999 or email hpa@23andme.com.
