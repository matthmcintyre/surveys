Why am I seeing this document?

This document serves as an addition to our general research consent form, and provides additional information specific to this particular survey. The general research consent form, which can be viewed here, includes a more complete summary of the risks and benefits of participating in our research.

The survey you are about to view is part of a research project to look for genetic correlates of alcohol use. This topic may be highly personal and difficult to discuss. Answering some of the survey questions may cause emotional discomfort. Similar to other, less sensitive surveys, you may receive information about the topic at the end of the survey, including general comparisons between your answers and those of other 23andMe participants. When other participants view general comparisons, your answers may be included if you choose to answer. In addition, scientific publication of genetic associations with specific behaviors or personal characteristics may change the way those behaviors or characteristics are perceived by the public. In some cases, such publication may affect the way you or groups to which you belong are perceived. The survey will most likely take between 5 and 20 minutes to complete.

As described in our research consent form, your self-reported information (stripped of any personally identifying information) may be shared in aggregate with external organizations, but your individual information will never be shared with third parties without your explicit authorization. To protect the privacy of your data, 23andMe uses a range of physical, technical, and administrative measures to safeguard your data, in accordance with current technological and industry standards. For more information, please see our privacy statement.

Participation in this research is completely voluntary, and you may stop answering this survey at any point, for any reason. Please note that due to the sensitive nature of this topic, persons under the age of 18 are not eligible for this study.

Whom do I contact if I have questions?

If you have any questions or concerns about this study, if you suffer a research-related injury, or if you have a question about subjects' rights, please contact the Human Protections Administrator at hpa@23andme.com.

What will you do with my data?

Your Genetic & Self-Reported Information will be stripped of personally identifying Registration Information and made available to a separate research environment. 23andMe investigators will then compare all participants' Genetic & Self-Reported Information to identify associations between specific genetic and non-genetic markers and traits, behaviors, or conditions.

23andMe may collaborate with external parties. Under this informed consent, external parties will only have access to pooled data stripped of identifying information. 23andMe will never release your individual-level data to any third party without asking for and receiving your explicit authorization to do so.
